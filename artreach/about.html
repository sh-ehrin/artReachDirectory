Our Story

About ArtReach

We are dedicated to strengthening the connection between New York City public high school students and the cultural institutions that make this city extraordinary.

Our Mission

Bringing Students Closer to Culture

ArtReach was created to make the extraordinary cultural wealth of New York City accessible to every student, regardless of borough or background. We believe that a field trip to a museum, theatre, or music hall is more than a school activity — it is an introduction to a lifelong relationship with the arts.


This directory is a practical, teacher-ready resource: every institution is organized by borough and type, with the information you need to plan a visit from start to finish.

🎨

Who Is This For

Built for NYC Educators

ArtReach is designed for New York City public high school teachers, department heads, and trip coordinators who want to incorporate cultural experiences into their curriculum without the administrative headache of searching across dozens of websites.


Whether you teach visual arts, music, English, or history — every institution in this directory offers programs relevant to your students.

How to Use This Directory +

ArtReach is organized by borough, then by institution type: Museums, Theatres, and Music Halls. Start on the home page and click the type of institution you're looking for under your borough. Each card shows you the essential information at a glance.


Click on any venue card to open its full detail page, where you'll find the address, hours, website, phone number, and a description of what the institution offers for student groups.


Use the Field Trip Guide to help with planning logistics once you've found your venue.

How to Plan a Field Trip +

Planning a successful field trip starts well before the day of the visit. Here is a general framework used by experienced NYC educators.


1. Choose your venue. Use this directory to find an institution that aligns with your curriculum. Check the hours and confirm they accept school groups.


2. Contact the education department. Most institutions have a dedicated school programs office. Call or email them early — some programs book out weeks in advance.


3. Submit your paperwork. Work with your school's trip coordinator to complete all required forms well in advance of the trip date.


4. Prepare your students. A pre-visit activity can dramatically improve engagement on the day of the trip.


See the full Field Trip Guide for more detail on each step.

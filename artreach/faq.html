Resources for Educators

Field Trip Guide

Everything you need to plan a smooth, curriculum-aligned cultural field trip for your NYC public school students.

Pro tip: Most NYC cultural institutions offer free or reduced-cost admission for public school student groups. Always call the education department directly before purchasing tickets — you may qualify for a fully subsidized visit.

Planning Your Trip

How far in advance should I book a field trip? +
Most institutions recommend booking 4–6 weeks in advance. For highly popular programs (such as those at Carnegie Hall or the Met), 8–10 weeks is advisable, especially for spring semester trips. Always confirm availability with the institution's education department directly.
What paperwork does my school require before a trip? +
Requirements vary by school and district. Generally you will need: a signed principal approval form, parent/guardian permission slips for all students, a completed DOE trip request form, and proof of transportation arrangements. Contact your school's trip coordinator early in the process.
How do I find out if an institution has school group programs? +
Every venue listed in ArtReach has a website link on its detail page. Look for an "Education," "School Groups," or "Group Visits" section. If you can't find it, call the main number and ask to be connected to the education department — they are almost always very helpful.

Day-Of Logistics

What is the recommended chaperone-to-student ratio? +
NYC DOE guidelines generally require one adult chaperone per ten students for high school trips, though individual institutions may have their own requirements. Always confirm with the venue ahead of time. Note that chaperones typically receive free admission.
What should students bring to a cultural institution? +
Students should bring their student ID, a notebook or sketchbook if appropriate to the activity, and any materials specified by the institution's education department. Encourage students to leave large bags at home if possible, as many venues have strict bag-check policies.
How should I prepare students before the visit? +
A short pre-visit lesson or discussion significantly improves student engagement and retention. Most institutions provide free educator resources and lesson plans on their websites. Even a 20-minute introduction to the artists, history, or themes students will encounter can make a meaningful difference.

Cost & Funding

Are there free admission options for NYC public school students? +
Many institutions offer free or deeply discounted admission for NYC public school groups. Programs like NYC Free Arts, the Cultural Institutions Group (CIG), and Title I funding can offset or eliminate costs. Always ask the education department about school group pricing before assuming admission is paid.
What funding sources can cover transportation costs? +
Transportation is often the biggest barrier for field trips. Possible funding sources include: your school's discretionary budget, parent-teacher associations, DOE Arts Counts grants, and direct grants from cultural institutions themselves. Some venues partner with the MTA or charter bus services to subsidize transportation for public school groups.
